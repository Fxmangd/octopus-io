Abstract & Background

Addressing the "Alzheimer's" of Multimodal Large Language Models.

Flexible as an Octopus 🐙

On the path to Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), enabling Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) to possess continual learning capabilities without suffering from Catastrophic Forgetting remains immensely challenging.

Traditional approaches face a dilemma: architecture-based methods endlessly increase parameters, while rehearsal-based methods rely heavily on historical data (raising privacy issues and storage overhead).

We introduce Octopus, a novel rehearsal-free framework featuring History-Free Gradient Orthogonalization (HiFGO). It accurately captures the "safe update direction" to avoid parameter interference without requiring any historical task data.

Achieving the perfect balance: "What memory to keep" vs. "What new to learn".

The core insight of Octopus is that orthogonalizing on the parameter level is far from enough; orthogonalization on the gradient level is the key to avoiding interference. More importantly, we can elegantly derive this "safe update direction" even in the complete absence of historical data.

2. Why GPWC works?

Theoretical validation of Gradients of Previous parameters Within Current data (GPWC).

How do we calculate sensitive gradients for old tasks without old data? We creatively utilize the "model parameters of historical tasks" to compute gradients on the "data of the current new task" as a proxy.

We theoretically prove: GPWC is mathematically equivalent to the projection of the old task's Hessian matrix onto the tangent space of the new task's data manifold.

3. Two-Stage Finetuning

A decoupled strategy inspired by simulated annealing: Learn fast, forget slow.

Stage 1: Unconstrained Adaptation

(Learn Fast) The model freely finetunes on the new task data without any constraints. This allows it to quickly absorb new knowledge and dive into the optimal local minimum for the current task, maximizing plasticity.

Stage 2: HiFGO Constraints

(Forget Slow) We re-introduce the HiFGO constraint for a second round of tuning. This gently "pulls back" the parameters into a safe manifold subspace that maintains high performance on the new task while avoiding conflicts with historical knowledge.

4. Experimental Results

Octopus establishes overwhelming dominance on the UCIT benchmark.

Comprehensive SOTA Performance

Octopus comprehensively surpasses previous methods (like O-LoRA, HiDe-LLaVA). Remarkably, as a Rehearsal-Free regularization method, it even outperforms methods dependent on actual historical replay data (Vanilla Rehearsal).

Positive Backward Transfer (BWT)

In a rare sight representing true "photographic memory", Octopus actualizes a positive backward transfer (BWT = +0.41), proving the model literally gets smarter horizontally without destroying past alignments.

Rock-solid Robustness

The core evaluation metrics remain optimal across a broad range of hyperparameters. Furthermore, regardless of randomized heterogeneous task orderings, the final performance remains consistently immune (Sequence Immunity).

Ultimate Inference Efficiency

Unlike MoE-based strategies that require loading massive redundant parameters as tasks increase, Octopus persistently maintains a single LoRA module, adding zero additional mathematical burden during inference ideal for edge devices.
