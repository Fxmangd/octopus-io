Challenge: Learning sequentially without catastrophic forgetting.

Abstract

Continual learning in multimodal large language models (MLLMs) aims to sequentially acquire knowledge while mitigating catastrophic forgetting. Existing methods face inherent limitations: architecture-based approaches incur computational overhead, rehearsal-based methods rely on storing historical data (raising privacy concerns), and conventional regularization is insufficient.

We propose Octopus, a two-stage continual learning framework based on History-Free Gradient Orthogonalization (HiFGO), which enforces gradient-level orthogonality without historical task data. Experiments on UCIT show that Octopus establishes state-of-the-art performance, surpassing prior SOTA by 2.14% and 6.82% in terms of Avg and Last.

✨ Key Contributions

  • HiFGO: Gradient orthogonalization leveraging only past weights—highly data-efficient & privacy-preserving.
  • Two-Stage Fine-Tuning: Decouples adaptation from regularization, beautifully balancing plasticity and stability.
  • Rehearsal-Free SOTA: Defeats even memory-replay methods without buffering a single past sample.

The Octopus Architecture

A graceful, two-stage finetuning strategy to prevent parameter interference.

Stage 1: Unconstrained Adaptation

During the first stage, the MLLM learns task-specific knowledge without constraints. This maximizes the model's plasticity, allowing it to fully adapt to the optimal representation for the new visual-linguistic task.

Stage 2: HiFGO Constraints

We apply History-Free Gradient Orthogonalization. By forcing parameter updates to be orthogonal to the sensitivity space of the previous model, we guarantee stability. Crucially, this uses 0% historical data!
